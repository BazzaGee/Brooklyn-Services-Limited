Design Specs: 

Overall Concept & Aesthetic
The design is strictly geometric, brutalist, and minimalist. It conveys stability, heavy industry, and structural integrity. Think "construction firm," "heavy machinery," or "architectural hardware." There are absolutely no curves, gradients, or soft edges—everything consists of hard 90-degree angles and solid, flat colors.
Composition
The logo consists of two distinct elements stacked vertically and separated by a clean band of whitespace.
1. The Top Element (The Primary Shape)
Shape: Imagine a solid, heavy rectangle that is wider than it is tall (roughly a 4:3 ratio). Now, visualize a "channel" or a "slot" cut horizontally into it from the left side.
Geometry: This creates a shape that resembles a squared-off, blocky letter "C" that has been flipped horizontally (facing left) or a heavy industrial clamp.
Stroke Weight: The top arm, bottom arm, and the vertical connecting block on the right side should all appear to have roughly the same heavy visual weight/thickness. The white negative space in the middle is a horizontal rectangle, slightly thinner than the red arms surrounding it.
Color: A vivid, opaque Scarlet or Fire Engine Red. (Think roughly hex #D81E28 or a standard Pantone Red 032).
2. The Bottom Element (The Base)
Shape: A solid, horizontal rectangle.
Dimensions: It has the exact same width as the red top element. However, it is much shorter vertically—roughly 1/4 to 1/3 the height of the top red shape. It looks like a heavy underline or a concrete slab foundation.
Color: A solid Dark Charcoal Grey. Not quite pure black, but very deep (approx. 80-90% Grey).
Spacing & Alignment
Alignment: The red top shape and the grey bottom bar must be perfectly flush-aligned on both the left and right edges. This creates a strong, invisible rectangular bounding box.
Whitespace: There is a distinct gap between the bottom of the red shape and the top of the grey bar. This gap should be clean and even, roughly equal to half the height of the grey bar itself.
Summary for Execution
Draw a red rectangle with a horizontal notch cut out of the left side. Beneath it, float a thinner dark grey rectangle of the exact same width. The result should look like an abstract, heavy-duty industrial component.
