# Rinnai vs Rheem: Which Hot Water System is Best for Your Home?

When it comes to hot water systems in New Zealand, two brands dominate the market: Rinnai and Rheem. As authorized Rinnai service agents for Canterbury, we install and service both brands daily. Here's our unbiased comparison to help you choose.

## Brand Overview

### Rinnai
- **Origin:** Japanese company with 100+ year history
- **NZ Presence:** Strong market leader in continuous flow
- **Specialty:** Gas continuous flow systems
- **Innovation:** Pioneer in tankless technology
- **Warranty:** Up to 12 years on heat exchanger

### Rheem
- **Origin:** American company founded 1925
- **NZ Presence:** Major player in all categories
- **Specialty:** Storage cylinders and continuous flow
- **Innovation:** Stellar and MPi series popular in NZ
- **Warranty:** Up to 10 years on cylinders

## Continuous Flow Systems Comparison

### Rinnai Infinity Series

**Popular Models:**
- **Infinity 16** - 16L/min, 1-2 bathrooms
- **Infinity 20** - 20L/min, 2-3 bathrooms  
- **Infinity 26** - 26L/min, 3+ bathrooms
- **Infinity 32** - 32L/min, large homes

**Key Features:**
- Puretemp™ temperature stability
- Compact design (60% smaller than 135L cylinder)
- 6-star energy rating
- Frost protection built-in
- 12-year heat exchanger warranty
- Wifi connectivity (some models)

**Pros:**
- Excellent temperature stability
- Industry-leading warranty
- Very reliable
- Lower running costs
- Compact size

**Cons:**
- Premium price point
- Requires 240V power (even gas models)
- Annual service recommended

### Rheem Stellar/Metro Series

**Popular Models:**
- **Stellar 330** - 16L/min, storage + continuous
- **Stellar 360** - Higher capacity
- **Metro 16** - 16L/min continuous
- **Metro 26** - 26L/min continuous

**Key Features:**
- Mains pressure delivery
- 5-star energy rating
- 10-year cylinder warranty (Stellar)
- Australian-made (Metro)
- Various capacity options

**Pros:**
- Stellar combines storage + continuous
- Good value for money
- Australian manufacturing
- Wide service network

**Cons:**
- Lower warranty period
- Temperature stability not as precise
- Less compact than Rinnai

### Our Verdict: Continuous Flow

**Choose Rinnai if:**
- You want the best temperature stability
- Warranty length matters
- Premium quality is priority
- You're installing new

**Choose Rheem if:**
- You want value for money
- Stellar's storage+continuous appeals
- Budget is a concern
- You prefer Australian-made

**Popular Models:**

- **Optima** - High-efficiency

- **MPi** - Heat pump series
- **Premier** - Stainless steel

- Various sizes (25L to 400L+)

**Pros:**

- Good value options
- All fuel types available
- Easy to find parts/service

**Cons:**

- Shorter warranties on basic models
- Standard efficiency on lower-end

### Our Verdict: Storage Cylinders

- You want premium build quality
- Solar compatibility matters
- Longer warranty preferred
- Gas heating priority

**Choose Rheem if:**
- You want variety and choice
- Budget is important
- Electric or heat pump needed
- You need specific sizing

## Installation Considerations

### Rinnai Installation

**Requirements:**
- 240V power connection (all models)
- Gas connection (natural or LPG)
- External wall mounting (typical)
- Ventilation clearances
- Annual service access

**Installation Complexity:** Medium
- Electrical work required
- Precise gas connection
- Professional installation essential

### Rheem Installation

**Requirements:**
- Varies by model type
- Some need 240V, some don't
- Various mounting options
- Clearance requirements

**Installation Complexity:** Low to Medium
- Generally simpler than Rinnai
- More flexible mounting
- Standard connections

## Service and Maintenance

### Rinnai Servicing

**Recommended:**
- Annual service for continuous flow
- Every 2 years for cylinders
- Professional service required

**Common Issues:**
- Scale buildup (less in soft water areas)
- Ignition issues (older models)
- Error codes (usually user-fixable)

**Parts Availability:**
- Excellent in NZ
- Authorized agents nationwide
- Good stock levels

### Rheem Servicing

**Recommended:**
- Annual service for continuous flow
- Every 3-5 years for cylinders
- Professional service required

**Common Issues:**
- Anode degradation (cylinders)
- Valve issues
- Age-related wear

**Parts Availability:**
- Excellent in NZ
- Wide service network
- Easy to find parts
