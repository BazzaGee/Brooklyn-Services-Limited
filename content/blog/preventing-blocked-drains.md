# How to Prevent Blocked Drains: 10 Essential Tips for Christchurch Homes

Blocked drains are one of the most common plumbing problems we encounter at Brooklyn Services. The good news? Most blockages are entirely preventable with some simple habits and regular maintenance. Here are our top 10 tips to keep your drains flowing freely.

## 1. Be Careful What Goes Down the Sink

**The Problem:** Kitchen sinks are notorious for blockages caused by food waste, grease, and oils.

**What Not to Put Down Your Sink:**
- Coffee grounds (they clump and stick to pipes)
- Cooking oils and grease (they solidify and coat pipes)
- Food scraps (even small particles accumulate)
- Flour and dough (it hardens like cement)
- Rice and pasta (they expand in water)
- Eggshells (they combine with other debris)
- Starchy foods (potato peels, rice)

**The Solution:**
- Use a sink strainer to catch food particles
- Scrape plates into the bin before washing
- Wipe greasy pans with paper towels before washing
- Collect cooking oil in a container for disposal
- Compost food waste where possible

**Pro Tip:** Once a week, pour boiling water down your kitchen sink to help dissolve any buildup.

## 2. Protect Your Shower and Bath Drains

**The Problem:** Hair and soap scum are the main culprits in bathroom drain blockages.

**Prevention Strategies:**
- Install a drain hair catcher (cheap and effective)
- Clean the hair catcher after every shower
- Brush hair before showering to remove loose strands
- Use less soap or switch to liquid soap
- Run hot water down the drain weekly

**Monthly Maintenance:**
Mix 1/2 cup baking soda with 1/2 cup vinegar and pour down the drain. Wait 15 minutes, then flush with hot water. This helps break down soap scum and hair.

## 3. Only Flush the Three Ps

**The Problem:** Toilets get blocked when people flush inappropriate items.

**Only Flush:**
- **P**ee
- **P**oo
- **P**aper (toilet paper only)

**Never Flush:**
- Wet wipes (even "flushable" ones)
- Paper towels
- Sanitary products
- Cotton balls or swabs
- Dental floss
- Hair
- Medications
- Food waste
- Cat litter
- Nappies

**Why It Matters:** These items don't break down like toilet paper and can cause major blockages in your pipes and the municipal sewer system.

## 4. Watch Your Garden

**The Problem:** Tree roots are a leading cause of outdoor drain blockages in Christchurch.

**Prevention:**
- Know where your drainage runs before planting trees
- Choose trees with less aggressive root systems
- Keep trees at least 3 meters from drainage lines
- Regularly inspect outdoor drains for root intrusion
- Consider root barriers for existing trees

**Warning Signs:**
- Slow-draining outdoor drains
- Gurgling sounds from drains
- Soggy patches in your garden
- Foul odors from drains

**Common Culprits in Canterbury:**
- Willow trees
- Poplar trees
- Oak trees
- Bamboo
- Large shrubs

## 5. Install Drain Screens

**Simple Protection:**
Drain screens or strainers are inexpensive and highly effective at preventing blockages.

**Types:**
- **Kitchen sink strainers** - catch food particles
- **Shower hair catchers** - trap hair before it enters pipes
- **Bathroom sink screens** - prevent small items from falling in
- **Outdoor drain grates** - keep leaves and debris out

**Maintenance:**
Clean screens regularly. A blocked screen can cause water to back up just like a blocked pipe.

## 6. Be Careful with Chemical Drain Cleaners

**The Problem:** Chemical drain cleaners can damage pipes and are often ineffective.

**Why We Don't Recommend Them:**
- They can corrode pipes over time
- They're harmful to the environment
- They often don't remove the entire blockage
- They can make the problem worse by hardening debris
- Dangerous if they splash on skin or eyes

**Better Alternatives:**
- Baking soda and vinegar
- Enzyme-based drain cleaners
- Hot water flushes
- Professional drain cleaning

**When to Call a Professional:**
If you have a persistent blockage, call a licensed drainlayer. We have equipment like water blasters and CCTV cameras that can properly diagnose and clear the problem.

## 7. Regular Hot Water Flushes

**Why It Works:**
Hot water helps dissolve grease, soap scum, and other buildup before it becomes a blockage.

**Weekly Routine:**
1. Boil a kettle of water
2. Pour it slowly down each drain
3. Follow with cold water for 30 seconds
4. Repeat for heavily used drains

**For Kitchen Sinks:**
Add a squirt of dish soap to the boiling water. The soap helps break down grease.

## 8. Maintain Your Gutters and Downpipes

**The Connection:**
Blocked gutters cause water to overflow and can wash debris into your stormwater drains.

**Maintenance Schedule:**
- **Spring:** Clear winter debris
- **Autumn:** Remove leaves (especially important in Canterbury)
- **After storms:** Check for blockages

**What to Look For:**
- Leaves and twigs
- Moss and vegetation
- Bird nests
- Cracked or damaged gutters
- Disconnected downpipes

**Pro Tip:** Consider installing gutter guards to reduce maintenance.

## 9. Know Your Drainage System

**Why It Matters:**
Understanding your drainage system helps you prevent problems and respond quickly when they occur.

**Know the Location of:**
- Your boundary trap (gully trap)
- Inspection openings
- Outside drains
- Septic tank or connection to main sewer
- Stormwater drains

**Keep Records:**
- Property drainage plans
- Previous plumbing work
- Maintenance history
- Professional inspection reports

**Emergency Knowledge:**
Know where your water main shut-off valve is. In an emergency, this can prevent significant damage.

## 10. Schedule Professional Inspections

**Prevention is Better Than Cure:**
Regular professional inspections can catch problems before they become emergencies.

**What We Check:**

## Signs of a Developing Blockage

Catch problems early by watching for these warning signs:

### Slow Drainage
- Water takes longer to drain than usual
- Multiple fixtures draining slowly
- Water pooling around drains

### Gurgling Sounds
- Strange noises from drains or toilets
- Bubbling in other fixtures when water drains
- "Glugging" sounds

### Bad Odors
- Sewer smells from drains
- Rotten egg smell
- Musty odors

### Water Backup
- Water coming up in other fixtures
- Overflowing drains
- Wet patches near drains
