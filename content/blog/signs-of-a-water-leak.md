# 7 Warning Signs You Have a Hidden Water Leak in Your Home

Water leaks can cause significant damage before you even realize there's a problem. As Christchurch plumbers with over 50 years of combined experience, we've helped thousands of homeowners detect and fix hidden leaks. Knowing the warning signs can save you thousands of dollars in repair costs.

## 1. Unexplained High Water Bills

**The Sign:** Your water bill suddenly increases without any change in your usage habits.

**What It Means:** Even a small leak can waste thousands of liters of water per month. A tap dripping once per second wastes approximately 11,000 liters per year—that's like running your dishwasher 400 extra times!

**What to Do:**
- Compare recent bills to the same period last year
- Check your water meter when no water is running
- If the meter is still spinning, you likely have a leak
- Call a professional leak detection service

## 2. Damp or Discolored Patches

**The Sign:** You notice wet spots, water stains, or discoloration on walls, ceilings, or floors.

**What It Means:** These are telltale signs that water is escaping from pipes behind surfaces. The leak may be slow and gradual, but the damage compounds over time.

**Common Locations:**
- Under sinks
- Behind walls with plumbing
- Ceiling below bathrooms
- Near hot water cylinders
- Foundation walls

**What to Do:**
- Don't ignore small damp patches—they indicate a larger problem
- Document the location and size
- Call a plumber before mold develops

## 3. Musty or Moldy Odors

**The Sign:** Your home develops a persistent musty smell, especially in certain areas.

**What It Means:** Hidden moisture creates the perfect environment for mold and mildew growth. Even if you can't see it, you can often smell it first.

**Health Concerns:**
- Mold can trigger allergies and respiratory issues
- Some molds are toxic
- Damp environments attract pests

**What to Do:**
- Investigate any persistent musty odors
- Check hidden areas like under sinks and behind appliances
- Professional mold remediation may be needed

## 4. Sound of Running Water

**The Sign:** You hear water running when all taps are turned off.

**What It Means:** This is one of the most obvious signs of a leak. The sound might be subtle—a faint hiss or trickle—but it indicates water is flowing somewhere it shouldn't be.

**When to Listen:**
- Late at night when the house is quiet
- After turning off all taps
- Near the water meter
- In walls or under floors

**What to Do:**
- Turn off all water sources completely
- Listen at various points in your home
- Check if the sound matches your water meter activity

## 5. Foundation Issues

**The Sign:** You notice cracks in your foundation, uneven floors, or doors that don't close properly.

**What It Means:** Underground leaks can erode soil beneath your foundation, causing settlement and structural damage. This is particularly concerning and needs immediate attention.

**Warning Signs:**
- New cracks in walls or floors
- Doors or windows that stick
- Uneven or sinking concrete
- Soggy patches in your garden

**What to Do:**
- This requires immediate professional assessment
- May need both plumbing and structural experts
- Document all cracks with photos

## 6. Low Water Pressure

**The Sign:** Your taps or shower have less pressure than usual.

**What It Means:** Leaks in your plumbing system can reduce water pressure. If it's affecting multiple fixtures, the leak is likely in your main supply line.

**Check:**
- Is pressure low throughout the house or just one area?
- Did the change happen suddenly or gradually?
- Are multiple fixtures affected?

**What to Do:**
- Check if neighbors are experiencing similar issues (could be municipal)
- Inspect visible pipes for leaks
- Call a plumber if the problem persists

## 7. Hot Spots on Floors

**The Sign:** You notice warm or hot spots on your floor, particularly if you have underfloor heating.

**What It Means:** Hot water leaks create warm spots as the water escapes into the surrounding area. This is common with slab leaks (leaks under concrete foundations).

**Common With:**
- Underfloor heating systems
- Hot water pipes in concrete slabs
- Homes built on concrete foundations

**What to Do:**
- Hot water leaks waste energy and money
- Professional detection is essential
- Thermal imaging can pinpoint the exact location

## What to Do If You Suspect a Leak

### Step 1: Confirm the Leak
1. Turn off all water sources in your home
2. Check your water meter
3. If it's still moving, you have a leak
4. Note the meter reading and check again in an hour

### Step 2: Try to Locate It
- Look under sinks and behind appliances
- Check for visible dampness or mold
- Listen for running water sounds
- Check your hot water cylinder

### Step 3: Call a Professional
DIY leak detection has its limits. Professional leak detection services use:
- **Acoustic equipment** to hear leaks
- **Thermal imaging** to see temperature differences
- **Moisture meters** to detect dampness
- **Experience** to interpret results

### Step 4: Document Everything
- Take photos of any damage
- Keep records of water bills
- Note when you first noticed symptoms
- This helps with insurance claims

## Prevention Tips

### Regular Maintenance
- Annual plumbing inspections
- Check under sinks quarterly
- Monitor water bills monthly
- Service hot water cylinders regularly

### Know Your Home
- Learn where your main shut-off valve is
- Know the location of all plumbing fixtures
- Understand your water meter
- Keep plumbing records

### Early Action
- Don't ignore small leaks
- Address dampness immediately
- Fix dripping taps promptly
- Call professionals for persistent issues
